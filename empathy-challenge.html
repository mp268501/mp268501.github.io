Compounding Empathy Challenge

Step inside the frustration students feel when the number system and the language around equality shift under your feet. Each task reuses the same six-letter digit set, so the discomfort compounds just like it does in the classroom.

Work through each activity. Pause when you feel the friction.

1. Base-Six Multiplication

Multiply the two alien numbers using long multiplication. Each cell only accepts the letter digits Z through E. Work deliberately so you can notice the friction.

  • Use the digit key for a quick letter ↔︎ number translation.
  • Finish one column at a time—carry on top, partial products in the middle, then the final product.

4. Proportional Reasoning Capstone

You are planning snacks for an alien exchange. Their recipe uses B scoops of sweet dust for every C scoops of grain. How much sweet dust keeps the taste right when you have AZ scoops of grain? Walk through each step using the letter S for the unknown amount. Your progress unlocks the next prompt.

  1. Step 1. Complete the proportion: B / C = ___ / AZ

    Keep sugar over grain on both sides. The letters from the multiplication exercise still apply.

  2. Step 2. Cross-multiply: B × AZ = C × ___

    Remember what you put in the blank above. If you switch to base ten, the balance tips.

  3. Step 3. Compute the left side product in alien digits.

    This is the same as multiplying B by AZ from the first exercise. Watch for carries.

  4. Step 4. Divide by C to solve for the unknown.

    You're almost there. Does your answer match the story the fractions were trying to tell?
